Schrödinger Equation Visualized
Quantum mechanics courses often introduce the subject in a typical rigorous mathematical style. This work aims to aid the student or hobbyist in visualizing the solutions to the Schrödinger equation.
These visualizations further facilitate an understanding of an already bizarre physical phenomenon.

Tools used: Python, numpy, scipy, MayaVi

Probability Density

Squaring each function within the box yields a probability density of the system. Each peak corresponds to the likelihood of finding the particle. If a human observer wishes to locate the particle, they must look at the highest probable area and avoid the valleys. Note that there are locations in the box where the particle will never be found. The following images are the above images with a squared wave function.


When nx remains static and ny increases, note that the number of peaks increase along the y-axis and remain statix along the x-axis
